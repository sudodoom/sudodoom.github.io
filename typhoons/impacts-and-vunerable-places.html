What are the impacts?

Impacts of a typhoon can vary.

Buldings:
High speed winds that slam into buildings can cause damage by either ripping apart certain sections
such as the roof. Another way in which wind can damage a building is by slamming other materials into the building

Vegetation:
High speed winds can pull out/up trees and other vegetation such as bushes or plants. The winds can snap branches,
deatch leaves, fruits, seeds, and flowers. Typhoons can also flood the ground which can cause damage by drowning the plants
Salt water from typhoons can kill vegetation as well as land slides due to soil erosion which happends because of lots of
rain.

Life: Typhoons impact many peoples lives. Typhoons can destroy houses, crops, kill animals, and ruin materials which leaves them with
little resources to use. Typhoons also impact peoples lives by ending them via debris or flooding/drowning. Typhoons can cut off
medical assistance off with destroying modes of transportation. Animals being killed or crops being destroyed.
